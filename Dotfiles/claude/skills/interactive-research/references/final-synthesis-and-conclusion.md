# Final synthesis — narrow to one conclusion

> Load this when you judge the space explored to the limits of value-return (fresh searches stop paying, or the human is satisfied) — pointed here from `re-reason-and-take-notes.md` — or when a single-answer first-pass sent you straight here. The notes-log format and certainty markers are defined in `SKILL.md`.

Synthesize supporting material from the turn notes (not from a re-dump of raw sources) and the human's direction/responses into `conclusion.md`; ensure you understand all their arguments, and aren't under-weighting something they mentioned in passing in favour of a body-of-research that was siloed into an irrelevant direction:

- the **answer / conclusion**, stated plainly, up front
- the reasoning, with **certainty markers** (+SURE / ~SUSPECT / -GUESS / --WONDER) on each load-bearing claim
- the **competing options and why-not** — show the frontier you narrowed across, for later re-weighing against evolving priorities
- **citations** throughout
- the **quarantine list** — the separated discoveries, as their own section for the human to triage (file as TODOs/tasks if that fits the project)

Put the most important content first and last; that's where your attention, and theirs, is strongest. Don't target a size — target correctness and relevance: some conclusions are one clear sentence, others need several headers. Reserve the returned-to-user *response* for meta-procedural communication, so its size never distorts the *answer*.

Before delivering, run `validate.sh` one final time (see *Tooling* in `SKILL.md`) — `conclusion.md`'s citations are the load-bearing ones, so every bracketed slug in it must resolve to a registered source.

## What "done" looks like

A defensible conclusion the human can act on, backed by a wider net than felt comfortable to cast, every load-bearing claim cited, graded, and confidence-marked — plus a clean, separate list of everything interesting you found and correctly refused to chase.
