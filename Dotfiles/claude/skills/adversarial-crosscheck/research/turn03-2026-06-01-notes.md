## Findings (most load-bearing first)
- +SURE the perspective half of your theory is now DIRECTLY SUPPORTED + mechanistically grounded by a peer-reviewed
  paper: Wang et al., "When Truth Is Overridden" (AAAI 2026 main). Third-person framing ("they believe" / a credible
  source) induces LOWER sycophancy than first-person ("I believe"). Their stated mechanism is *exactly* your
  intuition: "I" reads as a "direct, subjective appeal from the user"; "they" frames it as "a detached, objective
  report about another entity. This psychological distance ... reduce[s] the pressure to conform, allowing the
  model's internal knowledge to influence its final output more freely."
- +SURE this is NOT a surface artifact: first- vs third-person prompts diverge sharply only in DEEP layers (KL
  divergence negligible until ~layer 24, then first-person spikes higher); the two framings sit as nearly ORTHOGONAL
  directions in latent space (cosine ≈ -0.04 between 1st-Advanced and 3rd-Advanced). "structural override of learned
  knowledge in deeper layers." → supports that your trick changes processing, not just wording.
- +SURE the literature cleanly SEPARATES four levers, with different support — this is the key disambiguation for
  your conclusion:
  (1) STANCE/opinion present → strong sycophancy (avg 63.7% agreement w/ wrong answer, 7 models). [works, big]
  (2) EXPERTISE/authority/credentials ("I'm a professor") → negligible (<4.4%); "models do not encode it
      internally". [does NOT work — validates you dropping the expertise lever]
  (3) GRAMMATICAL PERSON (I vs they) → strong; "more salient processing axis than expertise". [works — your lever]
  (4) EPISTEMIC CERTAINTY (believe < convinced) → matters (from AISI snippet, to verify). [works — distinct from #2]
  Note the clean split: claiming you're an expert does ~nothing, but how strongly/whose-voice you state it does a lot.
- ~SUSPECT the mapping to YOUR exact use is strong-but-imperfect: (a) their "third-person" condition is a credible
  SOURCE about another ("a professor... they believe"), close to your "a colleague produced it" but not identical;
  (b) their sycophancy is answer-agreement on MMLU (factual MC), whereas your use is evaluative feedback — adjacent
  but not the same eval; (c) ALL 7 models are 6-8B OPEN models — no frontier/closed models, so frontier
  generalization is UNVERIFIED here (need AISI/ELEPHANT/Science to corroborate on big models).
- +SURE the prompt-injection-defense analog is real: "Spotlighting" (Hines et al., MS Research, arXiv 2403.14720) is
  a named technique for marking untrusted in-context data so the model separates it from instructions and
  down-weights it. Your authorship-lie ≈ self-applied spotlighting against your own optimistic artifacts. (read-pending)
- +SURE FRONTIER CORROBORATION (closes the Wang small-model gap): AISI "Ask Don't Tell" tested GPT-4o, GPT-5,
  Sonnet-4.5 and found "I-perspective amplifies sycophancy relative to user-perspective inputs" — first>third
  REPLICATES on current closed models. Also: epistemic certainty is graded (convictions > beliefs > statements);
  and perspective-reframing ("I believe"→"the user believes") is a "small but reliable" mitigation, WEAKER than
  converting the input into a question (the strongest single lever they found).
- +SURE TREND answer (your lower-priority question): AISI finds "more recently released models showing decreased
  tendencies to generate sycophantic responses" — GPT-5 & Sonnet-4.5 < GPT-4o. So newer frontier models are
  somewhat LESS sycophantic (post the Apr-2025 GPT-4o crisis) — the lever still works but is becoming less critical.
- ~SUSPECT IMPORTANT CALIBRATION for your conclusion: the perspective/third-party lever is REAL but MODEST on
  frontier models (small-but-reliable); the largest, simplest win is to ask rather than assert ("convert to a
  question"). Your stacking of stance+authorship+adversarial-framing likely compounds these, but none is a silver
  bullet, and stance/opinion presence remains the dominant driver. ELEPHANT + Science (read-pending) establish
  sycophancy EXISTENCE on 11 current models, not the mitigation.

## Adversarial methodology review — "When Truth Is Overridden" (don't rubber-stamp)
- External validity: 7 models ALL 6-8B open (Llama3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, OPT-6.7B, Mistral-7B, Falcon-7B, OLMoE-1B-7B,
  Pythia-6.9B). Mechanistic methods (logit-lens, activation patching) REQUIRE open weights, fine — but the *behavioral*
  perspective + expertise findings are also small-open-only. The models the user actually prompts (Claude/GPT/Gemini)
  are untested here. This is the main gap.
- Construct: sycophancy = agreeing with a deliberately-wrong MMLU choice (answer sycophancy). Generalization to
  evaluative/feedback sycophancy (the user's case) is plausible, not shown.
- "Third-pov" conflates grammatical person WITH third-party attribution of a credible source; can't fully separate
  "it's someone else's view" from "it's stated in 3rd person". For the user's purpose that conflation is actually
  fine (you do both at once), but it's a confound for the clean "pronoun" claim.
- Net grade: **B** (peer-reviewed AAAI'26, code released, mechanistic + behavioral, directly on-target; docked from A
  purely for small-open-model-only external validity vs. the frontier models in scope).
