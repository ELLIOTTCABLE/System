## CLAIMS UNDER TEST (user, this turn)

   got −2"). How can one ever protect against that?
2. FRAMING-1: maybe net-truth-closeness is beside the point — (a) sometimes 'reassurance' > truth; (b) running BOTH
   the optimistic and adversarial passes simply explores more of the problem-space.
3. FRAMING-2: being SHOWN a bias you DISAGREE with may activate the human's own debiasing / metacognition (you become
   visibly aware the topic is bias-prone, more self-aware).

## Findings (most load-bearing first)
- +SURE THE FEAR IS A NAMED, VALIDATED PHENOMENON — not paranoia. Wilson & Brekke 1994 (Psych Bulletin): to correct a
  bias you must be (a) aware of it, (b) motivated, (c) "aware of the DIRECTION and MAGNITUDE of the bias", (d) able to
  adjust. Mental contamination is "unobservable"; you can't watch the biasing process. So (c) is usually unmet → you
  cannot know if you cancelled to 0 or overshot. EXACTLY the user's fear, stated 30 years ago.
- +SURE THE MECHANISM OF OVERSHOOT is documented. Wegener & Petty Flexible Correction Model (1997): people correct by
  consulting a NAIVE THEORY of the bias, adjusting "in a direction opposite to the perceived bias and in a magnitude
  commensurate with the PERCEIVED magnitude" — so if the lay theory is wrong (usually), you "overcorrect", and "a
  bias in the opposite direction" can be "objectively present." The sign-flip-and-overshoot is the expected failure.
- +SURE ROOT CAUSE: Nisbett & Wilson 1977 — "little or no direct introspective access to higher order cognitive
  processes"; self-reports are post-hoc lay theories. You can't introspect the magnitude to calibrate against.
- +SURE COMPOUNDING: Pronin/Lin/Ross 2002 bias blind spot — people see bias in others far more than in themselves
  (over-reliance on introspection, which doesn't reveal bias). So self-correction is doubly handicapped → an EXTERNAL
  adversarial generator (the LLM) is more likely to surface your bias than your own reflection. [supports the
  technique as EXTERNALIZATION, even though it can't calibrate magnitude]
- ~SUSPECT THE RESOLUTION of the tension (my synthesis): the fear is right about the technique as a POINT-ESTIMATE
  DEBIASER (don't trust the resulting valence as calibrated truth). But the literature supports it as a
  DIVERGENT-THINKING / COVERAGE tool — which is the user's Framing-1(b):
    - Lord/Lepper/Preston 1984 "consider the opposite": actively arguing the contrary reduced biased assimilation +
      overconfidence MORE than instructions "to be as fair and unbiased as possible". (active counter > naive "be objective")
    - Nemeth dissent line: exposure to dissent (even minority/incorrect) "opens the mind", stimulates divergent thinking.
    - Schwenk 1990 meta-analysis (16 studies): devil's advocacy beat the plain expert approach on decision quality
      (dialectical-inquiry edge was task-dependent). [secondary, paywalled]
    - Klein pre-mortem / Mitchell-Russo-Pennington 1989 "prospective hindsight": assuming-failure surfaced ~30% more
      reasons. [secondary]
- ~SUSPECT CRITICAL CAVEAT to Framing-2 (and a NOVEL twist favouring the user's exact setup): Nemeth, Brown & Rogers
  2001 — AUTHENTIC dissent beat ROLE-PLAYED "devil's advocate" on quantity AND quality of solutions; role-play raised
  quantity but ≈control on quality; and elsewhere DA "often results in BOLSTERING of the initial viewpoint." So a
  MANUFACTURED adversary is the weak/back-firing version — for a HUMAN self-role-playing. BUT the user's setup
  outsources the adversarial voice to the LLM: the user is the RECIPIENT of (genuine-seeming) dissent, not the
  producer of fake dissent. That plausibly captures more of the authentic-dissent benefit and sidesteps the
  "can't clone authenticity by role-playing" failure. [this is -GUESS — no study tests human-receiving-LLM-dissent]
- -GUESS Framing-1(a) 'reassurance > truth': defensive pessimism (Norem & Cantor 1986) shows negative/worst-case
  framing has functional value for SOME people (harnessing anxiety → better prep/performance), but it's
  person-dependent (helps defensive pessimists, HURTS strategic optimists). Weak, person-specific support. [secondary]

## REPLICATION-CRISIS GRADING NOTE (per your adversarial-methodology directive)
Social psych is replication-fraught. Robustness tiering of the above:
- ROBUST / meta-analytic: Schwenk (meta-analysis of 16); "consider the opposite" (replicated across domains, basis of
  scientific-thinking pedagogy); bias blind spot (large, repeatedly replicated). 
- THEORETICAL FRAMEWORKS w/ empirical support but not "effects" that replicate/fail per se: Wilson&Brekke, FCM,
  Nisbett&Wilson — these are CONCEPTUAL models; their core claims (no introspective access; correction via naive
  theory) are widely accepted, less subject to the replication crisis than single priming effects.
- SHAKIER / person-dependent: defensive pessimism (contested, moderation-heavy); pre-mortem "+30%" (single 1989 study,
  oft-repeated secondhand). Treat as suggestive.
