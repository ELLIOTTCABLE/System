## Findings

The first-party Anthropic guidance is unusually concrete: numerical
limits (1,536-char cap, 500-line target, 64-char name limit), specific
field semantics (`when_to_use` as a Claude-Code extension), and a
strong stance on positive-framing as the #1 formatting steer. This made
seeding the criteria mostly straightforward — most criteria are direct
distillations of explicit Anthropic recommendations.

Peer-reviewed work on prompt-level negation mitigation is thinner than
practitioner discussion implies. The published papers document the
*phenomenon* (LLMs underweight negation tokens at the representation
level) and test fine-tuning fixes; affirmative-paraphrasing is
mentioned as related work but not isolated as a measured intervention.
The right intellectual posture: positive-framing is primarily
Anthropic-first-party guidance, with peer-reviewed work supportive on
the underlying mechanism rather than on the specific prompt-engineering
recommendation.

The Anthropic-published `skill-creator` SKILL.md is a higher-signal
artifact than third-party prompt-engineering blogs. It settled the
Markdown-vs-XML-examples question (Anthropic's own skill uses
Markdown). When refreshing, reading vendor-published example skills
directly is worth more than reading commentary about them.

The Opus 4.5+ over-triggering issue (P2) is genuinely a calibration
shift from earlier Claude generations; older blog-post advice that
emphasizes CRITICAL/MUST language is now somewhat counterproductive.
